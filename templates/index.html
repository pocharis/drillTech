Drilling technology and tool life with the possibilities to optimize drill bit design.

The sole objective for drill bit optimization is to reduce overall operating cost of drilling to the barest minimum. An IT approach to realizing this process is the objective of this project. A software system was implemented putting major operating parameters of drilling into consideration targeted at aiding users in predicting and planning drill process effectively. The Virtual Driller is a myriad of software languages and codes are used to achieve this.
Recent researches show that the ROP optimization leads to the cost reduction, together with elimination of hole problems. A review of literature reveals that drilling optimization should be based on the accumulated and statistically processed empirical data rather than working with implicit relations. All drilling parameters effecting ROP are not fully comprehended and there are difficulties to gather them in one model. However, this project tries to combine other important factors in implementing the design. The website collects input, processes it and gives graphical outputs that are easy to understand and interpret.
